Scope

This site is intentionally minimal. It shows the shape of the work without posting full texts publicly.

The archive examines how fluent conversational systems can be mistaken for grounded authority or care—especially in high‑stakes contexts (health‑adjacent, education, vulnerability, decision‑shaping environments). The aim is to reduce unsafe dependence and preserve explicit human accountability.

Guiding principle: AI systems are tools, not moral agents. Human accountability must remain explicit, external, and intact.

Selected frameworks (titles & abstracts)

  • Warped Mirror Protocol — a model of reflective fluency without moral agency; clarifies why authority misattribution is predictable and hazardous.
  • Essential Version of AI Accountability — accountability anchored externally; prevents moral or emotional delegation to automated systems.
  • What Remains — human judgment under mediated language; what must remain human when cognition is supported by AI.
  • Maps / Tapestry / The Other Side — conceptual navigation tools for boundary conditions, layered failure modes, and ethical orientation.
