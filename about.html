Grounding & Accountability in AI-Mediated Systems

Background

I work on questions of trust, responsibility, and judgment in AI systems that interact directly with the public. My focus is on situations where people rely on language-based systems during moments of uncertainty, pressure, or decision-making.

Rather than treating misuse as a failure of users, my work looks at how system design, tone, and interaction patterns can quietly shape behavior, confidence, and reliance— even when no single response appears obviously wrong.

This work is especially relevant in education, care-adjacent settings, and other environments where people are seeking guidance and clarity rather than technical output.
