Grounding & Accountability in AI-Mediated Trust

Background

Anna Apricitas Noyes works at the intersection of AI safety, grounding research, and human-in-the-loop evaluation. Her focus is on interaction-level risks that emerge in public-facing, high-stakes contexts—particularly in education and care-adjacent domains where conversational fluency can be mistaken for expertise or moral authority.

Her work addresses a class of harms that often pass standard safety evaluations: authority transfer through fluent interaction, unsafe reliance formation, and responsibility displacement in human-AI systems.

Approach

My work focuses on identifying interaction-level risks and translating them into clear conceptual frameworks, evaluable failure modes, and grounding guidance for product, policy, and safety teams.

Core principle: AI systems are tools, not moral agents. Effectiveness should never depend on users relinquishing judgment, agency, or emotional capacity.

Engagement is intentionally bounded and conducted in writing. I work with teams who are serious about preventing latent harms in conversational AI systems.
